Metacognitive Strategies

Strategies

Orient

  • Do you understand the problem?
  • What are the causes of the problem?
  • What information is relevant to solve this problem, and what information is irrelevant?
  • What are the key variables and how are they related?
  • What information is available to solve this problem?
  • Do you have any related experience with this kind of problem?
  • What is/are your goal(s) or sub-goals for solving this problem?

Plan

  • What are possible solutions for this problem?
  • What are the steps to solve this problem?
  • Which strategy or strategies will you use?
  • What information do you need to solve this problem?
  • What are the necessary elements of an acceptable problem solution?
  • Do you find the needed information to solve this problem?

Monitor

  • Are you reaching your goal?
  • Are you using the strategy that you chose?
  • Are you on the right track?

Evaluate

  • What are the reasons for proposing the solution?
  • Is your evidence appropriate to convince someone of your solution?
  • Did your solution meet the preset evaluation criteria?

Reflect

  • What did you learn from the problem?
  • Can you think of any other setting where those skills are applied?
  • Any other alternate solution?
  • Any pros and cons of the solution?
